How to build a resume that gets you HIRED!

21 Nov, 2024. Thursday

1. Include Linkedin profile in resume
As per research from ResumeGo, Including your LinkedIn profile in your resume can drastically increase your chance of landing an interview by 71%.
But unfortunately, only 48% of resumes include a linkedIn profile. You can try this tricky demand and supply kind of sale like this as they did in the movie Wolf Of Wall Street.

2. Include important keywords and skills
Use ATS friendly keywords which are relevant to your skills and specific job role. Avoid using cliche. Balance both hard and soft skills in description. (People usually neglect soft skills.)
Avoid using Crazy, Fluff and Buzzwords. Because they will do more harm than good.

3. Quantify your results
Use metrics to describe your achievements instead of using general sentences.
You can use X-Y-Z Resume Bullet Formula. Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z].
Eg: Increased QoQ sales 15% by using Straignt line sales system.

4. Ideal resume length
According to TalentWorks data, Keeping your resume in the range of 475 - 600 words increased the chance of getting hired by 2x.
77% of resumes are either under or over indexing this range. Moreover this range is the sweet spot for what hiring managers and recruiters want to see.
